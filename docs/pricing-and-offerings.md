# Pricing & Offering Buckets — LocalSync vs Yext

**Purpose:** Document Yext’s public pricing structure (competitive benchmark from Don), break down publisher tiers and vertical networks, and define LocalSync’s proposed modular offering buckets.

**Audience:** Don, product, sales  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Related:** [Yext vs LocalMap comparison](./yext-localmap-comparison.md)

---

## Executive summary

Yext sells **bundled per-location plans** ($199–$999/year) with publisher access tied to tier. Vertical networks (healthcare, legal, home services, etc.) are **not separately priced** for SMBs — access depends on plan and enterprise deals.

LocalSync’s competitive angle is **modular pricing**: customers pay for the listing tier and vertical add-ons they need, instead of upgrading an entire bundle for one industry network.

## Yext Listings — core plan structure

| **Emerging** | $199/yr | ~$16.58/mo | Basic citation cleanup | Secondary directories only (~30–40 publishers) |

**Enterprise:** Negotiated by location count and product mix. Typically includes API access, bulk management, org hierarchy, custom publisher integrations, and bundled Reviews / Pages / Analytics.

## Yext publisher tiers — what each plan unlocks

### Emerging ($199/yr) — “secondary only”

~30–40 directories. **Excludes** major consumer platforms.

Examples:

- MerchantCircle, eLocal, Local.com, EZLocal, Hotfrog, Tupalo
- Chamber directories, regional business directories

**Use case:** Cheapest citation footprint; not sufficient if Google/Apple/Yelp matter.

---

### Essential ($449/yr) — “minimum worthwhile” tier

Adds the listings that drive most local discovery:

| Platform | Notes |
|----------|--------|
| Google Business Profile | Primary local search surface |
| Apple Maps | iOS / Siri ecosystem |
| Bing Places | Microsoft search |
| Facebook | Social + local discovery |
| Yelp | Reviews + local search |
| Yahoo, MapQuest, TomTom, HERE Maps, Foursquare | Extended map/search graph |
| Waze | Where supported |

**Use case:** Standard SMB that needs the majors synced and monitored.

---

### Complete ($499/yr) — Essential + operations layer

Everything in Essential, plus:

- Enhanced listing synchronization
- Analytics dashboard (impressions, engagement by publisher)
- PowerListings+ (expanded publisher coverage)
- Duplicate suppression
- Better reporting

**Use case:** Agencies and businesses actively managing listing performance, not just presence.

---

### Premium ($999/yr) — Complete + reputation stack

Everything in Complete, plus:

- Review monitoring
- Review response workflows
- AI-powered reputation tools
- Advanced analytics & integrations

**Use case:** Multi-location brands or businesses where reviews are a core growth channel.

## Yext vertical publisher networks

Yext maintains **industry-specific publisher relationships**. For small businesses these are typically **bundled into higher tiers or enterprise agreements**, not sold as à-la-carte add-ons.

### Healthcare

**Publishers (examples):** Healthgrades, WebMD, Vitals, RateMDs, Zocdoc, Wellness.com, Sharecare, CareDash, Doctor.com, Medicare provider directories.

**Synced fields:** Physicians, clinics, hospitals, insurance acceptance, specialties, NPI-adjacent metadata.

---

### Legal

**Publishers (examples):** Avvo, FindLaw, Justia, Lawyers.com, Martindale-Hubbell, Nolo, BBB, local bar association directories.

**Synced fields:** Attorney profiles, practice areas, office locations, consultation methods.

---

### Home services

**Publishers (examples):** Angi, HomeAdvisor, Houzz, Thumbtack, Porch, HomeGuide, Nextdoor, BuildZoom, local contractor directories.

**Synced fields:** Service areas, licenses, emergency hours, appointment scheduling.

---

### Restaurant & hospitality

**Publishers (examples):** TripAdvisor, OpenTable, Yelp, Google, Apple Maps, DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub, menu platforms.

**Synced fields:** Menus, reservations, ordering links, holiday hours.

---

### Financial services

**Publishers (examples):** Bankrate, credit union directories, advisor directories, insurance directories, financial planning networks.

**Synced fields:** Advisor profiles, branch locations, products, compliance-oriented disclosures.

Modular structure — customers assemble what they need instead of jumping Yext tiers for one vertical.

## Unit economics — what our prices cover (July 2026)

Per-location monthly COGS at normal usage. Rounded, worst-case leaning.

| Cost driver | Used by | Est. cost /location/mo |
|-------------|---------|-----------------------:|
| Firecrawl crawls | Listing audits (~4 runs × ~10 URLs), public scans | ~$0.20 – $0.60 |
| AI Gateway tokens | Scan extraction, review reply drafts, FAQ drafts, audit comparison | ~$0.30 – $1.00 |
| Neon Postgres + pgvector | All data + embeddings | ~$0.10 amortized |
| Vercel (hosting, Blob, IndexNow pings) | App + visibility pages + photos | ~$0.15 amortized |
| Clerk (MAU + billing) | Auth, orgs, subscriptions — Clerk Billing takes **0.7%** of revenue + Stripe ~2.9% + 30¢ | ~$0.25 + ~3.6% of revenue |
| Resend / PostHog | Email + analytics | ~$0.05 amortized |
| **Total COGS** | | **~$1 – $2.25 + ~3.6% of price** |

**Gross margin by tier:** Basic $19 → ~88–92% · Premium $49 → ~93–95% · Pro $79 → ~95%+.
Heaviest users are Pro (more audits + AI drafts) but Pro also carries the most price. Vertical add-ons (+$15) are nearly pure margin until vertical API rails exist.

**Watch items:** public free scans are unmonetized COGS (~$0.05–0.10/scan) — acceptable CAC; cap per-IP if abused. AI reply drafts are the most variable token cost — hence the 3-free-then-Reputation gate.

## Where pricing shows in-product (surface map)

Principle: **manual is always free — pricing appears exactly where automation saves time.**

| Surface | Trigger | What it sells | Why there |
|---------|---------|---------------|-----------|
| `/pricing` (public) | Marketing nav | All tiers + vertical add-ons | Anchor vs Yext before sign-up |
| `/scan` results | Free scan completes | Sign-up → tiers | Score capped + locked findings create pull |
| `/dashboard/billing` | Sidebar nav | Clerk PricingTable (live checkout) | Single conversion point; banners deep-link here |
| Listings page banner | No `api_sync` feature | Premium | User is mid-manual-work — show the automated path |
| Publishers registry banner | No `api_sync` feature | Premium/Pro | Rails visibly labeled manual/audit-only |
| Reviews page banner | No `reputation` feature | Reputation add-on | Reply fatigue is the pain point |
| AI draft limit (action error) | 4th draft attempt without Reputation | Reputation | Value already proven by 3 free drafts |
| GBP push (action error) | Push without `api_sync` | Premium | Write-sync is the flagship automation |

Feature slugs in Clerk: `api_sync`, `analytics`, `ai_citation`, `reputation` — attached to plans in `billing.json`, checked via `getWorkspacePlan()` (`lib/billing/plans.ts`).
